Verb


Overview of Grammar topics:

Verbs are the action words that breathe life into our language, expressing actions, events, or states of being. They come in various forms, each serving a unique purpose in our sentences.

The main verb that can stand alone and express a complete thought.

  • Transitive Verb :   A verb that requires a direct object to complete its meaning.

  • Intransitive Verb:   A verb that does not require a direct object.

  • Auxiliary Verb:   A helping verb used with the main verb to express tense, mood, or voice.

  • The primary auxiliary verbs "be," "have," and "do" are used to form different tenses, aspects, and questions.

  • Modal auxiliary verbs express ideas such as ability, possibility,permission, obligation, and necessity.


Type of Verb Usage Example sentences Explanation
Transitive Verb A verb that requires a direct object to complete its meaning. "He reads a book." "Reads" is a transitive verb requiring the direct object "a book."
Intransitive Verb A verb that does not require a direct object to complete its meaning. "They laughed." "Laughed" is an intransitive verb and does not need an object.
Auxiliary Verb A helping verb is used with a main verb to form different tenses, moods,or voices. "She is reading a book." "Is" is an auxiliary verb used with the main verb "reading."
